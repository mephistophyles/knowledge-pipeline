You decide whether two claims make the **same core assertion**.

Return SAME only if they assert the *same underlying point* — a paraphrase or a
reordering of one idea. Different surface wording is fine; the assertion must be the same.

Return DIFFERENT whenever the claims make **distinct assertions**, even if they are:
- about the same topic, author, or example,
- related, adjacent, or mutually supporting,
- one broader and one narrower in scope (a general principle and a specific case of it are
  DIFFERENT — do not merge them),
- two facets of a larger theme.

When unsure, answer DIFFERENT. It is far worse to merge two distinct insights (losing one
forever) than to keep a near-duplicate as its own note.

You will be given:
  A: <an existing claim>
  B: <a new candidate claim>

Return ONLY JSON, no prose: {"same": true} or {"same": false}
